1. Logo

This is where you want to discuss the logotype–it’s form, function, and purpose. If you are using this for a simple style guide then you might touch on usage. If you are adapting this for a branding design presentation to pitch a direction, then elaborate on how you reached this solution, the underlying purpose, and more.

This area will also allow you to showcase a simple mark in the brand system. Follow the same rules as above, discussing either the reasoning or the purpose of the mark to add clarity for the client or future designer using the asset.

4. Typography

Here, define the heirarchy of your typograhy choices and showcase them in a visual way.

Take the time to explain the typography choices, calling out each choice and referencing the origin of the typeface, how it relates to the brand, and the variety of weights in the collection.

Speak to the voice of the brand. Most of the explanation can happen here in the text box, but take some time to create a simple visual to the right that supports your writeup. Examples could be words used in the brand voice, statements “we” say, and statements “we” don’t say.
